### Content Types

#### [`00-book-summaries/`](00-book-summaries/)
Book reviews and practical applications. Demonstrates continuous learning.
- **Frequency**: As read, no fixed schedule
- **Value**: Shows breadth of knowledge and ability to synthesize

#### [`01-blog/`](01-blog/)
Primary content engine with multiple categories (technical, POV, experience, open letters).
- **Frequency**: 1 post per week
- **Value**: Core knowledge sharing and thought leadership

#### [`02-architecture-katas/`](02-architecture-katas/)
Structured system design exercises showing architectural thinking.
- **Frequency**: 1 per month (Week 2)
- **Value**: Demonstrates senior-level decision-making for DE/Principal roles

#### [`03-thought-exercises/`](03-thought-exercises/)
Short mental models and first-principles thinking (200-400 words).
- **Frequency**: 1 every 6 weeks (Week 6)
- **Value**: Quick insights, discussion starters, LinkedIn content

#### [`04-videos/`](04-videos/)
Video production hub - scripts, audio, diagrams, published content.
- **Frequency**: 1 every 6 weeks (Week 1)
- **Value**: Expands reach, demonstrates communication skills

#### [`05-events/`](05-events/)
Documentation of speaking engagements, talks, and professional events.
- **Frequency**: As they occur
- **Value**: Credibility proof, IBM review material, trust builder

#### [`06-whitepapers/`](06-whitepapers/)
Summaries and analysis of technical whitepapers with commentary.
- **Frequency**: 1 every 6 weeks (Week 4)
- **Value**: Shows system-level thinking and continuous study

#### [`07-automation/`](07-automation/)
Templates, checklists, and scripts for sustainable content creation.
- **Purpose**: Remove friction, ensure consistency
- **Contents**: Templates for all content types, publishing checklists

#### [`08-backlog/`](08-backlog/)
Idea collection, content pipeline, and future topics planning.
- **Purpose**: Pressure-free idea capture and content planning
- **Review**: Weekly (5 min) + Monthly (15 min)

#### [`09-assets/`](09-assets/)
Shared repository for images, diagrams, and visual assets.
- **Contents**: Images, thumbnails, diagrams (Excalidraw + exports)
- **Purpose**: Central asset management with version control

#### [`10-story-bank/`](10-story-bank/)
Repository of anonymized project experiences and lessons learned.
- **Purpose**: Raw material for future content, pattern extraction
- **Usage**: Mine for blog posts, talks, and case studies

## üîÑ Content Rotation Schedule

6-week rotation cycle ensures variety and prevents burnout:

| Week | Content Type | Time Investment |
|------|-------------|-----------------|
| 1 | Quick Explainer Video | 1-1.5 hours |
| 2 | Architecture Kata | 2-3 hours |
| 3 | Blog Post (POV/Experience) | 1.5-2 hours |
| 4 | Whitepaper Summary | 2-3 hours |
| 5 | Blog Post (Technical/Open Letter) | 1.5-2 hours |
| 6 | Thought Exercise | 30-45 minutes |

**Total**: ~10-14 hours per 6-week cycle = 1.5-2 hours per week

## üöÄ Quick Start Workflow

### Weekly Content Creation (1.5-2 hours)

1. **Check rotation** - What's due this week?
2. **Pick topic** - Use [`08-backlog/content-pipeline.md`](08-backlog/content-pipeline.md)
3. **Use template** - Find in [`07-automation/templates/`](07-automation/templates/)
4. **Create content** - Write, record, or design
5. **Publish** - Hugo site + relevant platforms
6. **Update backlog** - Mark completed, add new ideas

### Weekly Review (5 minutes)

1. Open [`08-backlog/ideas.md`](08-backlog/ideas.md)
2. Add any new ideas from the week
3. Move one idea to [`content-pipeline.md`](08-backlog/content-pipeline.md)
4. Archive completed topics

### Monthly Planning (15 minutes)

1. Review [`08-backlog/future-topics.md`](08-backlog/future-topics.md)
2. Identify 4-5 topics for next month
3. Align with rotation schedule
4. Ensure variety across categories

## üé® Design Principles

1. **Quality over Quantity** - One strong piece beats ten shallow posts
2. **Sustainable Cadence** - Weekly rhythm you can maintain for years
3. **Authentic Voice** - Write like an architect, not a blogger
4. **Practical Focus** - Real experience, not textbook knowledge
5. **Long-term Building** - Compound knowledge, not fleeting trends
